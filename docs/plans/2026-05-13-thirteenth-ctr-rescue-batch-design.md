# Thirteenth CTR Rescue Batch Design

**Problem**

Several pages now have enough Google visibility to prove demand exists, but their click-through remains weak relative to the impressions they already receive. The issue is no longer basic indexation or page existence. The issue is that the title, description, and first-screen promise may still be underselling the exact search intent.

The strongest current candidates are:

- `azure-container-registry-pricing`
- `azure-event-hubs-pricing`
- `gcp-cloud-run-pricing`

These pages already have meaningful impressions and, in some cases, first-page visibility, so the next likely bottleneck is SERP appeal and immediate on-page intent confirmation rather than missing topical coverage.

**Decision**

Run a thirteenth narrow CTR rescue batch focused on the three pages above.

**Approach**

1. Preserve page roles, URLs, and overall structure.
2. Revisit title, meta description, and first-screen promise language.
3. Make the snippet and opening feel more like a direct answer to the exact pricing question the searcher typed.
4. Keep the edits local and avoid broad content rewrites.

**Pages in this batch**

- `src/pages/guides/azure-container-registry-pricing.astro`
- `src/pages/guides/azure-event-hubs-pricing.astro`
- `src/pages/guides/gcp-cloud-run-pricing.astro`

**Expected outcome**

These pages should better convert impressions into clicks by sounding more specific, more decision-oriented, and less interchangeable with generic provider pricing pages. A searcher should quickly see what each page helps them estimate, which billing drivers it clarifies, and why it is worth clicking instead of a generic documentation result.
